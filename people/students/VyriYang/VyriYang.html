CURRENT PROJECTS
Complex Data Representation

The project blends hybrid physical-digital experiences with data-driven storytelling, enabling users to explore multiple outcomes through interactive choices. Sound and motion elements amplify immersion, enhancing user engagement with dynamic narratives.

VisCIT: A Visualization-based Critical Incident Survey System

VisCIT is an interactive survey platform that combines critical incident methodology with dynamic visual feedback to elicit more accurate, context-rich responses. Through scenario-driven narratives and reflection prompts, it enhances recall and improves the reliability of self-reported data in complex domains.

Decoding Data Storytelling

Using AI-assisted clustering and manual coding, we identified two major categories—animation styles and practice—along with 11 subcategories and 49 classifications. Regression and correlation analyses revealed how different animations support comprehension and balance coherence with flexibility.

VYRI YANG

I'm currently a PhD researcher in the Division of Computer Science and Engineering at Louisiana State University. My research focuses on merging the artistic with the scientific to develop impactful, user-centered technologies that redefine the boundaries of human-computer interaction. My work spans across areas such as Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Narrative Visualization, Applied Machine Learning, and Data-driven Interactive Platforms, aiming to enhance user experiences and foster deeper engagement through innovative, data-driven designs, and narrative-driven technologies.
